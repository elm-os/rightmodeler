# Product Hunt launch research

- Researched: 2026-07-17
- Scope: current Product Hunt launch mechanics and official guidance for a
  developer-tool launch
- Source policy: Product Hunt's Launch Guide and Help Center only
- Status: research input, not a scheduled or published launch

## Executive recommendation

For a developer tool such as rightmodeler, the strongest Product Hunt launch is
not a leaderboard campaign. It is a clear, usable product launch that lets a
developer understand the value, see the workflow, and try it immediately.

The official featuring criteria prioritize products that are useful, novel,
high-craft, or creative. Product Hunt currently focuses on live digital products
and may not feature reports, services, directories, templates, or waitlist-only
pages. The launch should therefore present rightmodeler as a working developer
product, not as a report, consulting service, or future idea. The linked page
should provide immediate access, a direct primary action, and a credible product
walkthrough. [Source: Product Hunt Featuring Guidelines](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/9883485-product-hunt-featuring-guidelines)

Product Hunt explicitly says that the best day is the day the maker is most
prepared. If there are no timing constraints, the site's rule of thumb is to
launch at the start of its Pacific-time daily cycle so the product receives a
full day of visibility. Current Help Center documentation says scheduled posts
go live at 12:01 AM PST. [Source: Launch Guide](https://www.producthunt.com/launch/preparing-for-launch),
[source: How to post a product](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/479557-how-to-post-a-product)

## Current mechanics

### Account and eligibility

- [ ] Use a complete personal Product Hunt account. Company or branded accounts
      cannot post, vote, or comment.
- [ ] Confirm that the account can submit before setting a launch date. New
      personal accounts normally wait one week before gaining posting access. The
      current Help Center says subscribing to Product Hunt's newsletter provides
      immediate access if the maker wants to post sooner.
- [ ] Use a real first and last name, clear personal photo, useful headline or
      about section, and a non-company username. Incomplete or misleading profiles
      may receive more review or limited visibility.
- [ ] Self-hunt the product unless there is a concrete reason not to. Product
      Hunt recommends self-hunting, says a separate hunter is unnecessary, and says
      posting it yourself gives the maker full control.
- [ ] Add every participating maker by Product Hunt username before launch so
      they receive the Maker badge and can join the conversation as makers.

Sources: [posting access](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/481909-how-can-i-get-access-to-post),
[Community Guidelines](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/3615694-community-guidelines),
[how to post](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/479557-how-to-post-a-product),
[Hunter vs. Makers](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/10082986-hunter-vs-makers-and-how-to-change-them).

### Product eligibility and homepage featuring

- [ ] Launch a live digital product that people can use now. Product Hunt says
      it prioritizes currently available products and does not feature waitlist-only
      products unless immediate access is provided.
- [ ] Confirm that the linked website and onboarding work for a first-time
      visitor without private instructions from the maker.
- [ ] Describe the product's practical benefit and distinctive approach. The
      official featuring review considers usefulness, novelty, craft, and
      creativity. A product does not need to excel in all four.
- [ ] Avoid framing the launch as a report, service, directory, list, template,
      boilerplate, course, guide, or other category Product Hunt says it does not
      feature.
- [ ] Submit all launch content in English.

Product Hunt says featuring decisions are reviewed by its team and are usually
final. Engagement does not guarantee featuring. Reaching out is generally
unnecessary unless critical information was omitted from the submission.

Sources: [Product Hunt Featuring Guidelines](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/9883485-product-hunt-featuring-guidelines),
[unreleased-product guidance](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/484932-can-i-submit-an-unreleased-product),
[content removal guidance](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/3539992-why-was-my-comment-or-post-removed).

### Drafting and scheduling

- [ ] Start the submission from a personal account with `Post` or `Submit`, then
      provide the primary product URL.
- [ ] Use `Create Draft` until the complete launch has been reviewed.
- [ ] Schedule within 30 days of the desired launch date. Product Hunt's current
      scheduling article says a launch can be scheduled only within 30 days of the
      current day.
- [ ] Continue editing from `My Products` after scheduling.
- [ ] Keep the scheduled launch private while the team reviews it, or use the
      lock control on the scheduled page to make it viewable to anyone with the
      link.
- [ ] Expect the scheduled launch to be unindexed and unavailable for upvoting
      until it goes live.

Current Help Center articles say the final flow has two options: `Create Draft`
and `Schedule Launch`. A February 2025 article explicitly says `Launch Now` was
removed. Some pages in the older Launch Guide still mention `Launch Now`; those
references appear stale and should not be used for operational planning.

Sources: [How to schedule a post](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/2724119-how-to-schedule-a-post),
[Where did Launch Now go?](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/9823193-where-did-launch-now-go),
[How to share a scheduled launch](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/15706445-how-to-share-a-scheduled-launch).

## Submission content checklist

### Links and metadata

- [ ] **Primary URL:** Use the direct product, download, app-store, or repository
      page. Do not use a press article, blog post, shortened URL, or tracked URL.
- [ ] **Product name:** Use only the product name. Do not add a description or
      promotional language. Avoid emoji unless it is genuinely part of the name.
- [ ] **Tagline:** Prepare a plain, specific statement of what the product does.
      The official Launch Guide states a maximum of 60 characters and advises
      against gimmicks or exaggerated claims.
- [ ] **Description:** Explain the value proposition and important capabilities
      concisely. See the documented limit conflict below.
- [ ] **Topics or launch tags:** Choose only the strongest matches. The Launch
      Guide says up to three launch tags; the newer Help Center says to use a few
      closely related topics.
- [ ] **Pricing:** Select free, paid, or paid with a free trial or free plan.
- [ ] **Status:** Mark beta or not-yet-available status accurately, but do not
      rely on a waitlist-only launch if homepage featuring matters.
- [ ] **Product X account:** Add the product's account, not a personal account,
      if one exists.
- [ ] **Additional download links:** Add relevant App Store, Google Play, or
      equivalent product links if applicable.
- [ ] **Shoutouts:** The Launch Guide says a launch can include up to three tools
      that helped create the product. Verify that this field remains present in the
      live form before preparing copy for it.

Sources: [How to post a product](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/479557-how-to-post-a-product),
[Launch Guide content checklist](https://www.producthunt.com/launch/preparing-for-launch).

### Thumbnail

- [ ] Prepare a square thumbnail at the recommended `240x240` pixels.
- [ ] Keep the file below `3 MB`.
- [ ] If using a GIF, make the first frame work as a static thumbnail. GIFs
      animate on hover rather than automatically.
- [ ] Avoid strobing, rapid cuts, and unreadable text. Product Hunt may edit a
      GIF it considers unsuitable.

The official material does not state a required static-image format or a minimum
resolution. It also says animated thumbnails are not necessary for success.

Source: [How to post a product](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/479557-how-to-post-a-product).

### Gallery and demo

- [ ] Prepare at least two gallery images so the gallery is viewable.
- [ ] Export gallery images at Product Hunt's recommended `1270x760` pixels.
- [ ] Make the first image understandable without reading the rest of the page.
      For a developer tool, it should communicate the before-and-after outcome or
      primary workflow rather than showing a decorative brand slide.
- [ ] Arrange the remaining images as a short story: problem, workflow,
      evidence, and output. This sequence is a rightmodeler recommendation, not a
      Product Hunt requirement.
- [ ] If using video, upload it to YouTube before launch, use the full YouTube
      URL, and confirm it is not private. Product Hunt currently supports YouTube
      links only.
- [ ] Consider an interactive walkthrough for a workflow that is hard to explain
      in still images. The current Help Center names Arcade, Storylane, ScreenSpace,
      Hexus, Supademo, and Layerpath as supported Product Hunt gallery demos.

Product Hunt does not publish a video duration, aspect ratio, resolution, file
size, or social-card dimension in the official sources reviewed. Verify those
details in the live submission UI and the chosen YouTube or demo provider.

Source: [How to post a product](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/479557-how-to-post-a-product).

### Promo offer

- [ ] Decide whether a Product Hunt-specific offer helps a user try the product.
- [ ] If using one, prepare the offer wording, promo code, and expiration date.
- [ ] Ensure the offer rewards product use, not votes or Product Hunt activity.

Product Hunt permits a promo code in the posting flow and says the promo can be
added or edited after posting. It separately prohibits discounts, freebies, or
rewards offered in exchange for upvotes.

Sources: [How to add a promo code](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/4916443-how-to-add-a-promo-code),
[How do I share my post?](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/2690626-how-do-i-share-my-post).

### Maker's first comment

- [ ] Write the first comment before scheduling.
- [ ] Introduce the maker and the reason the product exists.
- [ ] State who the product is for and the main use cases.
- [ ] List the small set of features needed to understand the product.
- [ ] Explain what kind of feedback would be most useful.
- [ ] Mention pricing or the Product Hunt offer if relevant.
- [ ] Ask for feedback, questions, or opinions. Never ask for upvotes.
- [ ] Use a personal, humble, helpful tone with plain language. Avoid marketing
      copy and generic hype.

The official Launch Guide says the first comment appears below the gallery and
reports that 70 percent of products receiving a Product of the Day, Week, or
Month award included a maker first comment. This is correlation reported by
Product Hunt, not proof that a comment causes an award.

Source: [Launch Guide first-comment guidance](https://www.producthunt.com/launch/preparing-for-launch).

## Promotion rules

### Allowed and encouraged

- [ ] Share the direct Product Hunt launch link organically on social media.
- [ ] Announce the launch to an existing newsletter or user community without
      asking for an upvote.
- [ ] Share it in communities where the maker is already an active participant.
- [ ] Invite people to try the product, leave feedback, ask questions, comment,
      or share the launch.
- [ ] Respond to launch comments promptly and substantively.
- [ ] Use the product itself in a reply when that creates a useful demonstration.

### Prohibited or risky

- [ ] Do not ask anyone directly to upvote.
- [ ] Do not mass-message people by email, direct message, or another platform
      asking them to vote.
- [ ] Do not pay for votes, traffic, or a hunter.
- [ ] Do not incentivize votes with discounts, freebies, rewards, contests, or
      giveaways.
- [ ] Do not coordinate a voting group or use bots.
- [ ] Do not self-promote in unrelated Product Hunt comments.
- [ ] Do not use AI-generated comments. Product Hunt's current commenting rules
      explicitly ask for person-to-person conversation and prohibit LLM-generated
      comments.

Product Hunt warns that inauthentic activity can reduce ranking, remove a launch
from the homepage, remove the contribution, or restrict the account. The safe
call to action is: try it and tell us what you think.

Sources: [How do I share my post?](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/2690626-how-do-i-share-my-post),
[upvote guidance](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/484935-can-i-ask-my-community-friends-family-to-upvote-a-product),
[Community Guidelines](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/3615694-community-guidelines),
[Commenting Guidelines](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/10030102-commenting-guidelines).

## Launch-day checklist

- [ ] Verify the product URL, signup or install path, demo, pricing, promo, and
      analytics before the Pacific-time launch cycle begins.
- [ ] Publish the Product Hunt badge or embed on the product website after the
      launch is live.
- [ ] Publish the prepared social, newsletter, in-product, and community
      announcements with a feedback-focused call to action.
- [ ] Watch the Launch Day dashboard for position, upvotes, comments, and
      reviews.
- [ ] Reply to comments in real time or as close to it as possible. If multiple
      makers are involved, assign coverage shifts.
- [ ] Record product feedback, recurring questions, conversion signals, and
      broken onboarding steps throughout the day.
- [ ] Do not overreact to raw upvote counts. The ranking is dynamic and Product
      Hunt says one upvote does not always equal one ranking point.

Product Hunt says homepage rank considers featured status, engagement,
authenticity, and other undisclosed factors. The exact formula is intentionally
not published. [Source: How is the homepage ranked?](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/484938-how-is-the-homepage-ranked)

## Badges and embeds

- [ ] After launch, open the launch page's `Embed` action.
- [ ] Select the available theme and copy the generated code into the website.
- [ ] If the launch earns an eligible daily or weekly result, check whether the
      `Featured` or `Top Post` badge option is available.
- [ ] Remove or reposition the launch-day traffic badge when the website should
      return to its normal primary conversion path.

The official Help Center does not publish fixed badge dimensions in the article
reviewed. Use the generated embed rather than recreating it from assumed asset
specifications. [Source: How to add a Product Hunt badge to your website](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/2731371-how-to-add-a-product-hunt-badge-to-your-website)

## After launch

- [ ] Continue answering new comments and thank people sincerely.
- [ ] Follow up with users who tried the product and organize qualitative
      feedback for product planning.
- [ ] Claim or request access to the permanent Product Page.
- [ ] Track outcomes against the launch goals rather than using Product of the
      Day as the only success criterion.
- [ ] Continue promoting the Launch Page for up to two weeks if it remains
      useful, then transition ongoing promotion to the Product Page.
- [ ] If planning a future relaunch, wait at least six months and launch a
      significant update. Product Hunt says a minor UI or pricing change is not
      significant, and products sharing a root domain are subject to the same
      six-month gap.

Sources: [post-launch guidance](https://www.producthunt.com/launch/days-after-launch),
[relaunch guidance](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/484934-can-i-relaunch-my-product).

## Suggested launch goals for a developer tool

Product Hunt recommends setting measurable goals that reflect the product's
actual objectives. It lists traffic, users or leads, feedback, comments,
followers, sales, and reach as valid measures in addition to leaderboard rank.
[Source: Launch Guide goal setting](https://www.producthunt.com/launch/before-launch)

For rightmodeler, a useful scorecard would be:

| Goal                 | Suggested measurement                                                 |
| -------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Qualified awareness  | Unique Product Hunt referral visits to the product page               |
| Activation           | Visitors who reach the first successful analysis or install milestone |
| Developer intent     | Repository visits, installs, or other verified adoption event         |
| Learning             | Actionable feedback items and repeated objections or questions        |
| Conversation quality | Substantive Product Hunt comments, not raw comment count              |
| Retention signal     | Activated users who return after launch day                           |

These metrics are recommendations for rightmodeler. Product Hunt does not
prescribe their definitions or targets.

## Official-document conflicts and unknowns

Verify these items in the live submission form before finalizing assets:

1. **Description length:** The May 2025 Help Center article says the description
   is limited to 260 characters. The Launch Guide says 500 characters. Use 260
   characters as the safe production limit until the live form confirms the
   current value.
2. **Launch Now:** Current Help Center documentation says this option was removed
   and replaced by `Create Draft`; the older Launch Guide still mentions it.
3. **Topics vs. launch tags:** Official pages use both terms. The Launch Guide
   says up to three launch tags, while the Help Center recommends a few topics.
   Confirm the current label and selection cap in the live form.
4. **Scheduled launch time:** Current Help Center guidance describes selecting a
   date within 30 days and says posts go live at 12:01 AM PST. Older Launch Guide
   copy discusses choosing other times. Confirm whether the current scheduler
   exposes a time control.
5. **Static-image formats and file limits:** The reviewed official sources state
   the thumbnail size recommendation and a `3 MB` image limit, but do not list a
   complete set of accepted formats or gallery file-size limits.
6. **Video specifications:** Only YouTube, full URL, and non-private status are
   documented. Duration, aspect ratio, and resolution are not.
7. **Badge dimensions:** Not published in the official badge article. Use the
   embed generator.
8. **Social-card dimensions:** No official Product Hunt launch-guide dimension
   was found in the sources reviewed.
9. **Exact ranking formula:** Intentionally undisclosed to prevent gaming.
